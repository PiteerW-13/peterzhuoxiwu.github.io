Act 1.2 - The Diversity of the Earth's Outer Spheres

After understanding the inner layers of the earth, we decided to start observing the outer layers, so PETE controlled the spacecraft. As the soil outside the cabin slowly loosened, the spacecraft began to climb upwards. As the energy released by the spacecraft increased; the speed of the spacecraft also increased until it stopped on the surface.

After reaching the surface, PETE immediately began his explanation work.

"Sir, do you know which outer layers are included?"

I curled my lips confidently and answered: "This is not simple, it's just the atmosphere, hydrosphere and biosphere, what's so difficult about this!"

"Sir, your answer is not rigorous, there is also a lithosphere!" PETE immediately refuted.

I frowned and was puzzled by his rejoinder: "Doesn't the lithosphere belong to the inner layers?"

PETE shook his head and added: "In fact, the lithosphere is a layer between the inner and outer layers, which is transitional, including the top of the crust and the upper mantle, that is, the solid rock part above the asthenosphere."

Then let's talk about other spheres:

  • Atmosphere

    • The atmosphere is the gaseous envelope outside the Earth. It is the frontier for the exchange of matter between the Earth and the universe. According to the temperature changes of the atmosphere at different altitudes, it is usually divided into 5 layers (the specific content will be explained in the next section). The atmosphere is composed of gaseous substances surrounding the outermost part of the Earth. It is an indispensable material condition for the survival of humans and organisms. It is also a protective layer that keeps the surface temperature and moisture constant. Besides, it is an important driving force and medium for promoting changes in the surface morphology.

  • Hydrosphere

    • The hydrosphere is a generally continuous layer covering the surface of the earth composed of liquid water and solid water on the Earth's oceans and land. Its main body is the ocean, followed by surface water, groundwater, and water in the atmosphere. Under the influence of solar radiation, water circulation is constantly carried out. The water cycle not only regulates the climate and purifies the air, but also plays a very important role in material migration and energy conversion on the surface of the Earth (such as water scouring and dissolution)

  • Biosphere

    • The biosphere is the general term for all organisms on the Earth and their living environment. It does not occupy any space alone but is composed of gaseous substances on the outermost surface of the Earth that penetrate the hydrosphere, the lower layer of the atmosphere, and the lithosphere. It is an indispensable material condition for the survival of humans and organisms and is also a protective layer that keeps the surface at a constant temperature and moisture. It is also an important driving force and medium for promoting changes in the surface morphology.

    After listening to PETE’s introduction to the Earth's outer layer, I also gained a deeper understanding of the Earth. At the same time, I began to look forward to the next stop, and hurriedly asked him, "PETE, where are we going next?"

    PETE, like a calm and composed middle-aged man, rejoindered slowly: "Sir, I believe you will look forward to the next stop, because the next stop is to understand the Earth's atmosphere, which means we are ready to leave the Earth and explore other planets!"

    "Really?" I was so excited that I almost jumped up, "How long will we observe this time? I really hope to explore other planets as soon as possible!"

    PETE still answered slowly: "The importance of the atmosphere to the Earth is self-evident, so this time we may spend more time studying the atmosphere than the previous stops. I hope you can understand!"

    I scratched my head, smiled, and said, "It's okay. I am very lucky to have the opportunity to explore the universe."

    "Now the important thing is to quickly complete the observation of the next stop. Let's set off immediately!"
